CHICKEN BIRIYANI

Chicken biryani is a flavorful and aromatic South Asian dish that combines succulent pieces of chicken with fragrant basmati rice, spices, and herbs. Originating from the Indian subcontinent, biryani has various regional variations, each with its unique blend of spices and ingredients.

ingredients

  • 2 ½ tablespoons olive oil
  • 3 tablespoons plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons distilled white vinegar
  • 1 medium onion, sliced
  • 2 medium tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 small green chile peppers
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • 1 tablespoon ginger paste
  • 2 tablespoons garam masala
  • 1 tablespoon dried mint
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 5 sprigs cilantro
  • 1 (3 pound) whole chicken, skin removed and cut into pieces
  • 2 quarts water
  • 4 cups uncooked basmati rice
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 bay lea
  • 4 pods green cardamom
  • ¼ teaspoon powdered yellow food coloring

PROCEDURE


STEP 1

Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Stir in yogurt and vinegar. Add onion; cook and stir until golden and tender. Add tomatoes, chile peppers, garlic, and ginger paste. Cook and stir until tomatoes are tender. Season with garam masala, mint, salt, and pepper. Stir in cilantro.

STEP 2

Place chicken into tomato-onion mixture in the skillet. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and continue cooking, stirring occasionally, until chicken is no longer pink in the center and the juices run clear, about 45 minutes. An instant-read thermometer inserted into meat should read at least 165 degrees F (74 degrees C).

STEP 3

Meanwhile, bring water and rice to a boil in a large pot. Mix in cinnamon stick, bay leaf, green cardamom, and black cardamom. Season with salt. Cover, reduce heat to low, and simmer until rice is tender and water is absorbed, about 20 minutes. Discard cinnamon stick, bay leaf, and cardamom.

STEP 4

Spread 1/2 of the cooked rice in the bottom of a large serving dish. Pour chicken mixture on top. Cover with remaining cooked rice. Sprinkle with food coloring; mix to serve.

MUTTON BIRIYANI

Mutton biryani is a flavorful and aromatic South Asian dish that combines succulent pieces of Mutton with fragrant basmati rice, spices, and herbs. Originating from the Indian subcontinent, biryani has various regional variations, each with its unique blend of spices and ingredients.

ingredients

  • Seeraga samba rice – 1kg
  • Mutton – 1kg
  • Oil – 200grm
  • Onion – 400grm
  • Cinnamon – 1grm
  • Cardamom – 1grm
  • Clove – 1grm
  • Coriander Leaves – half hand
  • Mint Leaves – half hand
  • Ginger – 100g
  • Garlic – 50g
  • Red Chilli powder – 2 tablespoon
  • Salt – 2 tablespoon (For Masala)
  • Curd – 200ml
  • Green Chilli – 3
  • Tomato – 400grm
  • Water – 1 litre (For Masala)
  • Water – 3 litre (To Boil Rice)
  • Salt – 3 tablespoon (For Rice Boiling Water)

PROCEDURE


STEP 1

Combine mutton with curd, red chilli powder, salt, ginger paste, garlic paste, cinnamon, cardamom, and clove. Mix well and refrigerate for at least 2 hours or overnight..

STEP 2

Rinse the rice thoroughly until the water runs clear. Boil 3 liters of water with salt, then add the rice and cook until 70% done. Drain the rice and set aside.

STEP 3

Heat oil in a large pot and sauté finely chopped onions until golden brown. Add marinated mutton and cook until the meat is browned on all sides. Add finely chopped tomatoes, green chillies, red chilli powder, and salt, and cook until the tomatoes are soft. Add water and cook the mutton until it is tender.

STEP 4

In a large pot, layer the partially cooked rice over the mutton masala. Sprinkle finely chopped coriander and mint leaves over the rice. Drizzle some oil and a few drops of water over the top.

STEP 5

Cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes. Allow the biryani to rest for 10 minutes before opening the lid. Gently fluff the rice with a fork and serve hot.

BEEF BIRIYANI

Beef biryani is a flavorful and aromatic South Asian dish that combines succulent pieces of Beef with fragrant basmati rice, spices, and herbs. Originating from the Indian subcontinent, biryani has various regional variations, each with its unique blend of spices and ingredients.

ingredients

  • Beef – 1.5 kg
  • Unity Basmati rice – 1.5 kg
  • Onion – 600 grams (7 or 8 medium sized)
  • Tomato – 600 grams (7 or 8 medium sized)
  • Oil – 300 grams
  • Curd – 300 grams
  • Kashmiri Chilli powder – 3 tablespoons
  • Salt – 7 tablespoons
  • Clove – 3 grams
  • Cinnamon – 3 grams
  • Cardamom – 3 grams
  • Lemon – 1
  • Water – 1.5 litres (for masala)
  • Water – 5 litres (for boiling rice)
  • Green chilli – 5
  • Coriander leaves – 1 hand
  • Mint leaves – 1 hand

PROCEDURE


STEP 1

In a large bowl, combine the beef with curd, Kashmiri chilli powder, salt, ginger paste, garlic paste, and lemon juice. Mix well to coat the beef evenly with the spices and curd. Cover the bowl and refrigerate the marinated beef for at least 2 hours, or overnight for best results.

STEP 2

Rinse the Unity Basmati rice thoroughly under cold water until the water runs clear. In a large pot, bring 5 litres of water to a boil and add salt. Add the rinsed rice and cook until the rice is 70% done. Drain the rice and set it aside.

STEP 3

Heat oil in a large pot and sauté finely chopped onions until they turn golden brown. Add the marinated beef to the pot and cook until the meat is browned on all sides. Add finely chopped tomatoes, green chillies, and additional Kashmiri chilli powder if desired, and cook until the tomatoes become soft and form a thick masala. Add cloves, cinnamon, and cardamom, and continue to cook, stirring frequently. Pour in 1.5 litres of water and bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer until the beef is tender and the masala thickens.

STEP 4

In a large pot, layer the partially cooked rice over the mutton masala. Sprinkle finely chopped coriander and mint leaves over the rice. Drizzle some oil and a few drops of water over the top.

STEP 5

CCover the pot with a tight-fitting lid to seal in the steam. If the lid isn't tight enough, you can seal the edges with dough or a damp cloth. Place the pot on a tawa (flat griddle) or a low flame to create an indirect heat source. Cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes to allow the flavors to meld and the rice to finish cooking. Turn off the heat and let the biryani rest for another 10 minutes before opening the lid. Gently fluff the rice with a fork, ensuring the layers remain intact, and serve hot.
